RE: #186 - binary tree with 10 leaves - humblebeast - 09-09-2018

I think of it like this:

if you have 1 leaf, you have 0 nodes with 2 children.

in order to add 1 leaf, you must have 1 more node have 2 children (having 1 child just replaces, keeping the total number of leaves constant)

since you start with (leaves-1) nodes with 2 children, and you have to make 1 more node have 2 children to get a new leaf, you always have leaves-1 nodes with 2 children.
